Balayage is a new trend in hair coloring. What does it mean? And why should I care?


The balayage technique was developed in France in the 1980s. Today, it has become very popular around the globe. This method involves using light strokes from roots to ends to create a soft transition between dark and lighter shades.


This look is great for those who want to achieve a natural-looking result without having to spend hours at the salon. This type of hair coloring has become very popular over the last decade. In fact, it was named after the French word meaning “to lighten”. This method uses a special brush to apply a mixture of pigments to the roots of the hair.

Most women who choose balayage want their hair to look naturally colored. They don’t want to go through the hassle of bleaching their hair. Balayage allows them to achieve the same effect without damaging their hair.
